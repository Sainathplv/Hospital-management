FROM THE MD'S DSEK OUR HISTORY MILESTONES



For over years, we have tirelessly striven to live up to our vision and make a difference in the lives of our patients. A vision held by the pillars of transparency, confidentiality, ethics and integrity.




MILESTONES

  • Adopted 2 villages (Akkenapally and Nakrekal) in the district of Nalgonda.
  • Provided free medical care to rural areas and below economic segment in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Services provided are - Awareness Programs, Mobile Cancer Screening Facility, HIV/ AIDS Screening & Treatment, Surgical Procedures, Mother & Child Care, De-addiction programs.
  • Developed a Modern Urban Health Center in association with WHO.
  • We organize blindness prevention programs, cataract surgeries, fluorosis prevention programs, congenital correction program of cleft-lip & palate.
  • Partnered with Telangana State AIDS Control Society Hyderabad for integrated counseling and testing center (ICTC), where we conduct pre counseling, post counseling and free HIV test each day.
  • Partnered with AP State AIDS Control Society Hyderabad for Community Care Center (CCC), where we admit and treat for opportunistic infections. We provide medical services, medicine, food and beds free of cost.
  • We have setup urban and rural health centers at Nalgonda, where we give technical support, with per day outpatient count between 60 –70.
  • We have established de-addiction centers where we have special focus on rehabilitating and treating patients with alcohol and drug addictions. Per day ‘outpatient’ count is 4 – 5 and ‘in-patient’ is 25. We enjoy a success rate of 97% while providing care at very economical prices.
  • Every alternate day ´Dental Screening Camps´ are conducted at various villages with an outpatient count of 80 – 90.
  • Every Thursday, ´Fluorosis Camps´ are conducted at several villages with an outpatient count of 40 – 50.
  • Empanelled with the District Blind Control Society, where we conduct 15 free cataract surgeries per day.
  • Organized mega health camp for more than 3,500 tribals of Achampet, Mahaboobnagar district.
